# SCORING_RUBRIC.md

Use this rubric to keep idea scoring more consistent.

## Categories

Score each category from 1-10.

### 1. Pain Severity
How badly does this problem hurt?
- 1-3: mild annoyance
- 4-6: meaningful inconvenience
- 7-8: repeated operational drag
- 9-10: strong financial/reputational pain

### 2. Frequency
How often does the problem occur?
- 1-3: rare / one-off
- 4-6: occasional
- 7-8: weekly recurring
- 9-10: daily or always-on

### 3. Buyer Clarity
How obvious is the buyer?
- 1-3: unclear
- 4-6: broad but fuzzy
- 7-8: identifiable role or business type
- 9-10: precise buyer with clear use case

### 4. Willingness to Pay
How likely is payment if solved?
- 1-3: weak monetization
- 4-6: possible but uncertain
- 7-8: plausible paid product
- 9-10: obvious ROI / budget line

### 5. Workaround Ugliness
How ugly is the current solution?
- 1-3: current workaround is fine
- 4-6: somewhat inefficient
- 7-8: clearly painful/manual
- 9-10: obviously broken or embarrassing

### 6. Incumbent Weakness
How weak are current alternatives?
- 1-3: good solutions already exist
- 4-6: solutions exist but are imperfect
- 7-8: gap is real
- 9-10: market is wide open or badly served

### 7. MVP Simplicity
How quickly could a meaningful MVP exist?
- 1-3: large / complex build
- 4-6: moderate scope
- 7-8: focused MVP possible
- 9-10: very tight MVP possible

### 8. Distribution Accessibility
How reachable is the audience for validation?
- 1-3: hard to reach or opaque
- 4-6: possible but expensive/difficult
- 7-8: reachable through known channels
- 9-10: easy to reach where they already talk

## Interpreting the Score

This rubric is directional, not mathematical law.

A rough interpretation:
- **8.5-10** — very strong candidate
- **7.0-8.4** — promising, worth tracking
- **5.5-6.9** — weak/moderate, likely needs more proof
- **below 5.5** — probably not worth design time

## Important Note

A single fatal weakness can outweigh a decent average.

Examples:
- great pain but no reachable buyer
- nice concept but weak willingness to pay
- interesting workflow but incumbent solutions already good enough

Use judgment, not blind arithmetic.
