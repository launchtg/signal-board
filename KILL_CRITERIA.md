# KILL_CRITERIA.md

This document exists to prevent zombie ideas.

## Principle

Not every researched idea deserves Design.
Not every designed idea deserves Validation.
Not every validated idea deserves Build.

Killing weak ideas is part of keeping Signal Board useful.

## Kill or Deprioritize an Idea When

### Research-stage failures
- the buyer is still unclear after research
- the pain is too weak or too infrequent
- the evidence is thin or one-off
- the market already has good-enough solutions
- the problem is interesting but not monetizable

### Validation-stage failures
- traffic is relevant but conversion is poor
- signups come from the wrong audience
- people like the idea but do not want it enough
- replies are polite but low-intent
- no one asks for launch timing, access, or pricing
- willingness-to-pay signals are absent

### Build-stage failures
- validation looked good but implementation scope exploded
- acquisition channels are weaker than expected
- the product is too costly to deliver at the expected price point
- adjacent opportunities become clearly stronger

## Hold Instead of Kill When

Sometimes an idea is not dead — just early.

Hold if:
- the pain seems real but distribution timing is bad
- the audience exists but the messaging is weak
- the concept is promising but positioning needs work
- the market may be seasonal or timing-sensitive

## Signs of a Zombie Idea

Watch for these:
- it stays on the board because it sounds clever
- no one wants to remove it because of sunk effort
- validation data is weak but people keep rationalizing it
- the board has more emotion than evidence around the project

## Default Rule

If evidence is weak, do not promote the card.

Signal Board should get clearer over time, not more crowded.
