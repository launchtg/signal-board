# NewFilings — Brand Guide

## Brand Core

**Brand name:** NewFilings  
**Category:** Fresh business filing alerts for first-mover outreach  
**Audience:** Insurance agents, accountants, payroll providers, merchant services, and SMB sales teams  
**Core promise:** Reach new businesses before your competitors do.

## Positioning

NewFilings helps operators act on newly formed businesses while the opportunity is still fresh.

This is not a bloated sales-intelligence suite.
It is a timing advantage.

## What the brand should communicate

- speed
- sharp commercial advantage
- credibility
- modern operational clarity
- selective access / insider edge

## Emotional tone

The product should feel like:
- early
- premium
- focused
- useful
- high-conviction

It should **not** feel like:
- clip art
- playful startup fluff
- generic SaaS template design
- noisy dashboard software
- enterprise compliance sludge

## Messaging Principles

### Lead with the payoff
Do not lead with monitoring, scraping, or filing databases.
Lead with what the buyer gets:
- earlier outreach
- better odds of winning the account
- less time wasted on stale leads

### Keep copy tight
Use short sentences.
Use strong claims.
Cut filler.

### Direct-response angle
The page should answer:
1. Why should I care?
2. Why now?
3. Why is this better than how I do it today?
4. Why should I join the waitlist?

## Voice

**Voice:** direct, modern, credible, commercially aware  
**Style:** concise, founder-led, sharp, benefit-first

### Good examples
- Reach new businesses before your competitors do.
- Fresh filings are one of the cleanest sales signals in the market.
- Get there first. Close earlier.
- Stop waiting for stale lists.

### Avoid
- streamline your workflow
- unlock synergies
- AI-powered intelligence platform
- end-to-end solution
- transform your operations

## Visual Direction

### Overall feel
- premium light theme
- restrained, modern, editorial product aesthetic
- clean grid
- generous whitespace
- clear typography hierarchy
- product-first composition

### Reference qualities
Think:
- modern software startup
- selective beta product
- product team with taste
- polished but not flashy

Not:
- template marketplace landing page
- marketing agency fluff page
- cartoon SaaS brand

## Color System

Use the palette shown in `brand-guide.png` as the visual reference.

Working direction:
- deep ink / navy for anchors and headings
- muted blue-gray neutrals for structure
- warm white / soft off-white backgrounds
- one controlled green accent for action states and emphasis

### Color behavior
- backgrounds should stay light and calm
- accent color should feel intentional, not sprayed everywhere
- avoid rainbow UI
- avoid neon overload

## Typography

Use the type direction shown in `brand-guide.png`.

Recommended system from the selected guide:
- **Display / headlines:** Satoshi
- **UI / body:** Inter

### Typography rules
- headlines should be bold, tight, and decisive
- body copy should be clean and highly readable
- use size contrast aggressively to create flow
- avoid too many weights

## Icons

Use strong, modern **stroke icons**.

### Icon rules
- consistent stroke weight
- minimal detail
- geometric, clear silhouettes
- no clip-art style fills
- no emoji-like friendliness

Icons should support concepts like:
- alerts
- speed
- filings
- watchlists
- targeting
- first access

## UI Direction

The product UI should feel:
- real
- operational
- selective
- useful

### Show in mockups
- watchlist matches
- newly filed businesses
- priority alerts
- clear action states
- simple filtering or routing logic

### Avoid in mockups
- meaningless charts
- fake KPIs with no business meaning
- random analytics widgets
- dense enterprise admin clutter

## Landing Page Direction

### Required structure
1. strong benefit-led hero
2. one clear product visual
3. short sections explaining the business advantage
4. selective-access waitlist framing
5. strong final CTA

### Page should feel like
- a real startup with momentum
- a product insiders want early access to
- a premium niche tool with a sharp reason to exist

### Page should not feel like
- a feature dump
- a long explainer
- a validation survey
- a generic SaaS page

## Waitlist Framing

Preferred CTA language:
- Join Waitlist
- Request Early Access
- Get Priority Access
- Reserve Access

Preferred framing:
- private rollout
- early access
- priority queue
- selective onboarding
- first access
